## Survey Results & Analysis

### 1. Current Role of Respondents

- **Undergraduate students:** 78.1%
- **University faculty (lecturer/professor):** 12.5%
- **Postgraduate students (Masters/MPhil):** ~3%
- **PhD researchers:** ~3%
- **Other:** ~3%

**Insight:**  
The primary target users are undergraduate students, indicating that ScholarFlow should strongly focus on usability, simplicity, and learning-oriented workflows.

### 2. Primary Field of Study / Work

**Insight:**  
A strong technology-centric audience, validating the feasibility of technical features such as automation, smart organization, and AI-assisted research workflows.

### 19. Concerns or Hesitations (Open-Ended Summary)

**Key recurring concerns:**

- Data privacy & transparency
- Cost / subscription fees (student affordability)

- Plagiarism & academic integrity

**Insight:**  
Adoption depends heavily on trust, clarity, and ethical AI usage.

### 20. Additional Feature Requests (Open-Ended Summary)

**Common themes:**

- Collaborative study rooms (Docs + Discord + Miro style)
- AI-powered exam/question generator

- Class schedule & task automation
- LMS integration (Google Classroom, Moodle, UIU UCAM)
- Smart bookmarking & collections
- Topic-based paper recommendations with tracking

**Insight:**  
Users view ScholarFlow not just as a paper manager, but as a complete academic productivity ecosystem.

### 21. Additional Comments, Suggestions, or Feedback

**Total responses:** 10 (open-ended)

#### Primary Themes Identified:

**1. Overall Positive Reception**  
Most respondents view ScholarFlow as a good initiative with strong potential, especially because students struggle with resource management and staying organized.

**2. Emphasis on Simplicity & Control**  
Multiple responses stress that success depends on a clean, simple, and controlled design, rather than feature overload.

**3. Pricing Sensitivity**  
Affordable, student-friendly pricing is considered critical for adoption.

**4. Focus on Real Student Pain Points**  
Users highlighted notes management, past exam materials, and reminders as high-impact areas.

**5. Recommendation for MVP-First Approach**  
Launching as a minimum viable product (MVP) targeted at a single university or department was recommended to gather actionable feedback before scaling.

**Insight:**  
Users are supportive of the idea and believe ScholarFlow can succeed if it prioritizes usability, reliability, affordability, and phased rollout over complexity.
